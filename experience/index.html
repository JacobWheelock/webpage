Research Teaching Programming

January 2022 - May 2023

Brain Dynamics and Control Research Group

During the spring of 2022 I completed my honors thesis under Dr. ShiNung Ching by developing a real-time transcranial stimulation platform from the ground-up beginning with prototyping on a breadboard and eventually creating and printing a PCB to house the platform. The following summer I was funded under a Research Experience for Undergraduates (REU) grant to develop a pilot experimental paradigm for the platform to verify its efficacy. Our findings were then presented the following spring at the NER 2023 conference. At this conference I presented a scientific poster detailing the electronic specifications of the stimulator and the most recent experiment run.


January 2021 - April 2022

Stream-Based Supercomputing Lab

During my time in the SBS lab, I used the programming language CUDA to parallelize the Compton-scattering reconstruction algorithm and allow it run on a GPU. This work resulted in a first-authorship on a publication that I spoke about at the Urgent HPC workshop at Supercomputing 2021. Following this, I worked on developing novel ways to quickly and accurately cluster gamma-ray burst events in a different part of the same Compton-scattering reconstruction pipeline.
