January 2022 - Ongoing

Neural Dynamics and Control Research Group

During the spring of 2022 I completed my honors thesis under Dr. ShiNung Ching by developing a real-time transcranial stimulation platform from the ground-up beginning with prototyping on a breadboad and eventually creating and priting a PCB to house the platform. I now work on using this platform to develop a closed-loop neural feedback system in the pursuit of better understanding the effects of electrical stimulation on working memory.


January 2021 - April 2022

Stream-Based Supercomputing Lab

During my time in the SBS lab, I used the programming language CUDA to parallelize the Compton-scattering reconstruction algorithm and allow it run on a GPU. This work resulted in a first-authorship on publication and I spoke at the Urgent HPC workshop at Supercomputing 2021 to deliever my findings. Following this, I worked on developing novel ways to quickly and accurately cluster gamma-ray burst events in a different part of the same Compton-scattering reconstruction pipeline.
